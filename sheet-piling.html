What is Sheet Piling?


The term sheet piling is often encountered when discussing excavation and excavation work.
But what is sheet piling? Sheet piles are support structures, typically made of steel, concrete, wood,
or HD polyethylene, used to resist soil pressure during excavation work. Usually, sheet piling serves
as a temporary structure to minimize ground movement, allowing for excavation within a specified area.
When working in water, such as during bridge construction or demolition, sheet piling is used around the
work area to facilitate water removal. There are several different types of sheet piling, depending on soil
conditions, whether the excavation takes place in water, or if the ground is particularly rocky and hilly.
